How It Works

The EcoTrack is designed to help you understand the impact of your daily activities on the environment. By entering data about your lifestyle, you can estimate your total carbon emissions and find ways to reduce them.

Step-by-Step Guide

  • 1. Enter Your Transportation Details: Input the kilometers traveled using different modes of transport like petrol/diesel cars, buses, trains, and walking or cycling.
  • 2. Record Your Energy Usage: Add your electricity, natural gas, and solar energy consumption to calculate energy-related emissions.
  • 3. Choose Your Diet Type: Select your diet (vegan, vegetarian, or non-vegetarian) to account for food-related carbon emissions.
  • 4. Calculate Your Footprint: Click the “Calculate” button to view your total carbon footprint based on your inputs.

How We Calculate Emissions

We use globally recognized average emission factors to estimate your carbon footprint:

  • Transportation:
    • Car (Petrol): 0.192 kg CO₂/km
    • Car (Diesel): 0.171 kg CO₂/km
    • Bus: 0.089 kg CO₂/km
    • Train: 0.041 kg CO₂/km
    • Bicycle/Walking: 0 kg CO₂/km
  • Energy Usage:
    • Electricity (grid average): 0.475 kg CO₂/kWh
    • Natural Gas: 0.185 kg CO₂/kWh
    • Solar/Wind: 0 kg CO₂/kWh
  • Diet:
    • Vegan: 2.89 kg CO₂/day
    • Vegetarian: 3.81 kg CO₂/day
    • Non-Vegetarian: 5.63 kg CO₂/day

Understanding Your Results

Your total carbon footprint is calculated by adding emissions from transportation, energy usage, and diet. The result provides a snapshot of your impact on the environment, helping you identify areas for improvement.

Next Steps

Once you have your results, explore our Tips to Reduce Carbon Footprint page to find actionable ways to lower your impact.
